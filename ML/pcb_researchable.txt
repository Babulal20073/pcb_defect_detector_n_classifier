✅ 1. PROBLEM WITH EXISTING PCB DEFECT DETECTION PROJECTS

(Short, clear, perfect to show your ML teacher)

Most existing PCB defect detection systems — especially student projects and even many research papers — only implement one simple module:

✔ YOLO-based object detection to locate defects

This approach has four major weaknesses:

⚠ Problem 1 — They detect defects even in non-PCB images

If a random image (grass, football field, tiles) is uploaded, YOLO still predicts “holes” because:

The system has no verification step

No classifier to check whether the image actually contains a PCB

⚠ Problem 2 — They only detect, but do NOT evaluate severity or risk

Most papers output:

bounding box

defect type

confidence

But industrial PCB inspection requires much more:

criticality of defect

severity score

risk level

impact on board quality

Existing systems do NOT offer this.

⚠ Problem 3 — They don’t generate a complete inspection report

Most research stops at:

“Here is the detection result.”

Companies need:

PDF reports

metadata

defect tables

annotated images

audit trail

This is missing in almost all student and research projects.

⚠ Problem 4 — No multi-stage intelligence (single-model only)

Most projects run only YOLO.

But YOLO alone struggles with:

new defect types

small datasets

ambiguous regions

uncertain predictions

They never combine:

classifier

ensemble models

anomaly detection

quality scoring

explanation maps

Your project will solve this.
